[**American TOP 40**](/intellirdb?v=charts&sv=at40)

December 27th weekend 2025.

Claiming the #1 spot for the year was Lady Gaga and Bruno Mars' duet _'Die With A Smile,'_ a collaboration that captured hearts with its timeless ballad quality and emotional depth, proving that superstar pairings still resonate in today's music landscape.

At #2, newcomer Alex Warren's _'Ordinary'_ made an extraordinary impact, while Myles Smith's _'Stargazing'_ secured #3, marking a breakthrough year for both emerging artists who turned introspective storytelling into mainstream success.

The pop powerhouse Sabrina Carpenter once again dominated the year-end countdown with an impressive five entries, the most of any artist in 2025. Her chart presence included _'Taste'_ (#6), _'Espresso'_ (#9), _'Bed Chem'_ (#16), _'Manchild'_ (#19), and _'Please Please Please'_ (#35), showcasing her versatility and staying power in an increasingly competitive landscape.

Billie Eilish continued her reign with _'Birds Of A Feather'_ landing at #5, while her album track _'Wildflower'_ also cracked the top 40 at #39, demonstrating her ability to turn deeper cuts into mainstream hits.

Rising stars made significant waves this year. Chappell Roan's _'Pink Pony Club'_ hit #4, becoming an anthem for self-expression and LGBTQ+ pride, while Gracie Abrams' _'That's So True'_ claimed #10, cementing her status as one of pop's most compelling new voices.

Hip-hop made its presence felt with Kendrick Lamar earning two spots: _'Luther'_ featuring SZA at #11 and the cultural phenomenon _'Not Like Us'_ at #34. SZA herself appeared three times, including _'30 For 30'_ with Kendrick (#32) and _'BMF'_ (#37).

Shaboozey's country-rap fusion continued to blur genre lines, with _'A Bar Song (Tipsy)'_ holding strong at #13 and _'Good News'_ at #26. Morgan Wallen's collaboration with Tate McRae, _'What I Want'_ (#25), further proved that country crossovers remain a defining trend.

Bruno Mars appeared twice in the top 10 through collaborations—with Lady Gaga at #1 and Rosé's viral sensation _'APT.'_ at #7—proving his Midas touch for turning features into gold.

Hozier's _'Too Sweet'_ maintained momentum from its 2024 breakthrough, landing at #8 this year, while legacy artists like Justin Bieber (_'Daisies,'_ #22), The Weeknd (_'Cry For Me,'_ #15), Ariana Grande (_'Twilight Zone,'_ #27), and Ed Sheeran (_'Azizam,'_ #40) reminded us that star power endures.
